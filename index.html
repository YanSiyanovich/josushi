助数詞

助数詞っていったいなんだよ

助数詞じょすうし or measure words are used to count things. In English we don't say three advices. Instead we say three pieces of advice. We also don't say two breads, instead we say two loaves of bread or two slices of bread. Japanese counter words work in a similar way (except, unlike English, there is a ton of them).

While in English you can say two rooms, in Japanese you would say 二つの部屋 (two "things" of rooms).
